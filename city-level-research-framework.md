# City-Level Research Framework for USA Laser Directory

## Why City-Level Search is Superior to State-Level

### 1. **Local Intent Signals**
When potential customers search for laser engraving or sign services, they typically include their city or metro area in the query. A search for "laser engraving Los Angeles" indicates strong purchase intent from the LA area, whereas "laser engraving California" is more generic and may include irrelevant results.

### 2. **Local SEO Optimization**
Businesses optimize their online presence for their specific geographic location. This means:
- City-specific keywords in website content
- Google Business Profile listings tied to specific neighborhoods
- Local directory citations (Yelp, Yellow Pages city pages)
- Reviews mentioning specific locations

### 3. **Improved Recall**
State-level searches often miss:
- Local specialists who serve specific neighborhoods
- Smaller shops that don't rank statewide
- Newer businesses without broad web presence
- Local franchises of national sign companies

### 4. **Geographic Business Ecosystems**
Each city has its own business community:
- Industrial zones host specialized engravers
- Design districts have creative sign companies
- University areas have student-focused trophy/award shops
- Downtown areas have corporate-focused printers

### 5. **Practical Outreach**
For Trade Etching Direct's sales team:
- City-level data enables territory planning
- Regional targeting for trade shows and direct mail
- Better prioritization of high-potential metros

## Target Metro Areas by State Size

### Large States (CA, TX, FL, NY)
**Primary Metros (5-8 cities):**
- State capital
- 2-3 largest population centers
- Major suburban/edge cities
- Emerging growth corridors

### Medium States (PA, OH, IL, GA)
**Primary Metros (3-5 cities):**
- State capital + 1-2 major cities
- Significant suburban markets

### Small States
**Primary Metros (1-3 cities):**
- State capital
- One major metro area

## Research Methodology

### Step 1: Identify Target Cities
For each state, compile list of cities based on:
- Population (>100k for primary, >50k for secondary)
- Industrial/commercial presence
- Existing directory coverage gaps

### Step 2: Search Query Templates

**For Engravers:**
- `laser engraving [city] [state]`
- `laser cutting [city] [state]`
- `industrial laser marking [city] [state]`
- `custom awards trophies [city] [state]`

**For Distributors:**
- `Epilog laser distributor [city] [state]`
- `Trotec laser dealer [city] [state]`
- `laser machine sales [city] [state]`

**For Sign Companies:**
- `custom sign company [city] [state]`
- `metal plaques [city] [state]`
- `laser cut signs [city] [state]`

### Step 3: Data Collection Fields
For each candidate, capture:
- Company name
- Full address or city location
- Website URL
- Phone number
- Email (if findable)
- Social media (LinkedIn, Instagram)
- Category (Engraver/Distributor/Sign Company)
- Main brands carried (for distributors)
- Quality indicators (certifications, years in business, portfolio)
- Notes on specialization

### Step 4: Deduplication Strategy
**Normalization Rules:**
1. Company name: lowercase, remove LLC/Inc/Corp suffixes
2. Website: extract domain, remove www/https
3. Phone: strip all non-digits
4. Address: standardize city names

**Merge Criteria:**
- Same normalized company name + same domain = duplicate
- Same normalized company name + different domain = verify manually
- Different names but same phone/address = potential rename

### Step 5: Quality Scoring
Rate each entry 1-10 based on:
- Has website (yes +2)
- Has email contact (+1)
- Has phone (+1)
- Has LinkedIn (+1)
- Has Instagram (+1)
- Industrial specialization (+2)
- Portfolio/case studies (+1)
- Years in business mention (+1)
